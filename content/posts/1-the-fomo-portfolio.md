I was on the fence about whether I actually needed a portfolio site. Most tech folks have one because there's so much "cool shiz" happening in the field right now, but since I'm no longer a techie, I didn't want to geek out just for the sake of it.

The pressure wasn't just about building it, it was the content.

Do I even have that many thoughts?
Am I just doing this out of #fomo?
Does a personal site even make a difference anymore? (okay, you may ask "difference to whom?" and we could go on a philosophical tangent about it but leaving that for another day!)

Finally, I bit the bullet. Not because I had a burning desire to code, but because I wanted an answer for the next time someone asks: *"What's the most recent thing you taught yourself?"*

I started with the basics. My techie friends vehemently argued I should go straight to Claude Code, but I chose Framer. I wanted a tool that matched my vision, and not overwhelm the fudge out of me. I built a couple of drafts using the default templates on Framer but didn't quite work for me. I was obviously resistant to the discomfort of building from scratch, but eventually, I gave myself a pep talk and caved.

I used Claude as my design partner. I fed it my vision (aka "the prompt"), the specific colours I wanted (always been a fan of violet), and how I wanted the flow to be! Voila, a site I actually love. There are 1000s of lines of code under the hood, 999s of which I do not understand but I probably typed fewer than 100 words to configure it. If that isn't magic tech, IDK what is.

**The breakdown:**

- **Platform:** Framer (for the structure and hosting)
- **Logic:** Claude (for the custom components)
- **Content:** Straight from the heart and brain (no AI-generated fluff here and if that isn't obvious, it means AI has reached a level where it mimics actual humans who don't talk fluff!)

It turns out, being a generalist doesn't mean you can't or shouldn't build technical things.

It just means you're smart enough to use the best tools to get there (And that's how you self-pat yourself!)
